Atomic Habits

James Clear

Habits are compound interest of self improvement.

For better results, forget about setting goals, focus on system.

Problems with goals
1. Winners and loosers have same goals
2. Achieving goals is only a momentary change
3. Goals restrict happiness
4. Goals are at odds with long term progress

- 3 layers of behavior change - outcomes, processes, identities.
- The more pride you have in particular aspect of your identity, the more motivated you will be to maintain the habits associated with it.
-whatever is your identity right now, you only believe it because you have proof of it. Small habits can make a meaningful difference by providing an evidence of new identity.
-Ask, "who is the type of person that could get those results?"
- process for identity change
1.Decide the type of person you want to be.
2.Prove it to your self with small wins

-Feedback loop behind human behaviour - try, fail, learn, try differently.
-Habit can be broken down into a feedback loop that involves four steps: cue, craving, response, reward.
- 4 laws of behavior change
1. Make it obvious
2. Make it attractive
3. Make it easy
4. Make it satisfying

- Pointing and calling raises your level of awareness from a non-conscious habit to more a conscious level by better actions.
- Habits Scorecard technique.
- People who make a specific plan for when and where they will perform a new habit are more likely to follow through.
- Implementation intention: I will {behaviour} at {time} in {location}.
- Habit stacking: After {current habit}, I will {new habit}.

If you want to make a habit a big part of your life, make the cue a big part of your environment.
- Avoid mixing of contexts of one habit with another ex- Chair for reading, focus on desk.
- Stop thinking of environment as filled objects, start thinking as filled with relationships.

- To eliminate bad habit, reduce exposure to cue that causes it.

-Dopamine is released also when you anticipate pleasure.

We need to make habits attractive as expectation of reward motivates us to act in first place.
-Temptation Building: Strategy to pair action you want to do with an action you need to do Habit Stacking + Temptation Building:
1. After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [HABIT I NEED].
2. After [HABIT I NEED], I will [HABIT I WANT].

We tend to imitate the habits of three social groups: the close, the many, the powerful.

-Reframing habits to highlight their benefits rather than drawbacks is a fast way to reprogram mind and habits.
- cause of habit is prediction that proceeds them. The prediction leads to feeling.
- create a motivation ritual by doing something you enjoy before a difficult habit.
- Best is the enemy of the good.
- Focus on taking action not motion.
- Amount of time performing a habit is not as important as the number of times you have performed.
- Business is a never ending quest to deliver same result.
- addition by subtraction- Japanese lean production.
- remove points of friction with habits.

-Two-Minute Rule- when you start a new habit, it should take less than two minutes to do it.
- if this feels forced, then stop exactly at 2 min.
-the more you ritualise at beginning of habit, the more you can slip into focus.

- Commitment device is a choice you make in present that locks in better behaviour in the future.
- Use Tech to automate decisions.
- Technology creates a level of confidence that enables you to act on your smallest whims and desires.

-The more immediate pleasure you get from an action, the more strongly you should question whether it aligns with your long term goals.
-Cardinal Rule of behaviour change - what is immediately rewarded is repeated, what is immediately punished is avoided.
-Make avoidance(frivolous spending)visible and reward(like add money to buy jacket a/c) for its substitute.
-To get a habit to stick you need to feel immediately successful- even if its a small way.

- Marking progress is satisfying especially with visual measures like moving marbles, pins, Habit tracker.
- Never miss twice.
-An accountability partner can create an immediate cost to inaction.
-A habit contract can be used to add social cost to any behavior.
- The areas where you are genetically predisposed to success are the area where habits are more likely to be satisfying.
- Google famously asks its people to spend 80% of workweek on official job and 20% on projects of choice.
-Finding favoured habits:
1. What feels fun to me but work to others?
2. What makes me lose track of time?
3. Where do I get greater returns than the average person?
4. What comes naturally to me?

- Play the game that favours your strengths. If you can't find a game that favours you create one by specialisation.
- The Goldilocks Rule states we experience peak motivation when working on tasks that are right on their edge of current abilities.
- slight dip in motivation, leads to seeking novelty and derailing.
- variable rewards amplify craving.
- Pros stick to schedule, amateurs let life get in way.
- Whenever you're looking to improve, rotate through 4 laws of change until you find next bottleneck.
- "Happiness is the space between one desire being fulfilled and a new desire forming". And suffering is between craving a change in state and getting it.
-"He who has a why to live for can almost bear any how" == Great craving can power great action.
- Satisfaction = Liking - Wanting.
